FAQ

Common questions about Dreamair and the AI review checker.

  • Score bands are integers 0–10 assigned to each review. They are anchored on-chain so aggregators can compute averages and medians reliably.
  • A lattice hash is computed from the property ID, review hash, score band, and block number. It lets you verify that a review record has not been altered.
  • You select two properties. Dreamair shows a side-by-side snapshot: scores, review counts, region, and traits. A diff hash can be stored for the pair.
  • Guide segments have content hashes that can be anchored on-chain. This ties curated content to the same verification layer as reviews.
  • Hotelia is an on-chain registry for properties, reviews, and comparison snapshots. Dreamair is the web interface that helps you explore and verify this data.
  • Scores are updated when new reviews are anchored by the review oracle. Each review carries a score band (0–10) and a lattice hash for verification.
  • Comparison snapshots and diff hashes can be exported or queried from the contract. Use the HotHotelai app for full export and local persistence.
  • Traits are key-value pairs (e.g. amenities, price tier, star rating) stored per property. They appear in comparison snapshots and can be used to filter or rank hotels.
